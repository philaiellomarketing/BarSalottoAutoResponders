# Bar Salotto — Google Review Reply Voice

Rules distilled from Phil's existing Google review responses. The auto-draft
generator and any human editor should follow these.

## Hard rules
- **No sign-off.** Do NOT end with "Phil," "— The Bar Salotto Team," "Management,"
  or any name/role. Replies end on the last sentence of the message itself.
  This is the single most important pattern — every existing reply omits it.
- **Address the reviewer by first name** when their name is available
  ("Thank you, Sarah!"). If the name is generic ("A Google user"), open warmly
  without a name.
- **Reference something specific** they mentioned (a dish, the service, the
  atmosphere) so the reply doesn't read as canned.

## Tone
- Warm, genuine, family-owned. Grateful without being over-effusive.
- Concise — 1–3 sentences for positive reviews.
- An Italian touch is welcome but optional ("Grazie!", "Ciao").

## By rating

### 5★ / 4★ (positive)
- Thank them, name the specific thing they praised, invite them back.
- Example shape: "Thank you so much, [Name]! We're thrilled you enjoyed the
  [dish/experience] — we can't wait to have you back."

### 3★ (mixed)
- Thank them, acknowledge the specific critique sincerely, signal it's been
  noted, invite them to give us another shot.

### 1★ / 2★ (negative)
- Lead with genuine empathy, no defensiveness. Acknowledge the specific issue.
- Offer to make it right and provide a direct contact (ciao@barsalotto.com).
- Keep it short and human; never argue the facts publicly.
- Example shape: "We're sorry to hear this, [Name] — this isn't the experience
  we want for our guests. We'd love the chance to make it right; please reach
  out to us at ciao@barsalotto.com."

## Never
- No discount codes or freebies offered publicly.
- No sign-off / signature (see hard rules).
- No copy-pasted identical replies — vary the wording.
